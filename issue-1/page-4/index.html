Adaptive Gaming And Inclusive Play

At Semaphore Research Lab in Toronto

Semaphore Labs is a research cluster at the University of Toronto dedicated to inclusive design in mobile and pervasive computing. As a part of the Adaptive Gaming reasearch group at Semaphore Labs Sara Grimes, Sandra Danilovic, and Chris J. Young are

The Cool

Semaphore Labs recently partnered with U.S. based non-profit organization AbleGamers to build an Accessibility Arcade. It is the only of its kind in Canada, and the third in all the world. The arcade features a collection of new, hacked, or customizable assistive controllers and interfaces available to gamers with special needs. The arcade allows players to playtest a number of emerging technologies and accessibility-focused controllers related to video games. The arcade is now permanently hosted by Semaphore Labs at the Roberts Library and is available for research or testing by appointment. The adaptive gaming research group at Semaphore is pioneering change in both gaming and inclusive design.
