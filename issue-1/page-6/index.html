Co-Working and Collaboration in Games

At Bento Miso in Toronto

Bento Miso started in 2012 as a community and co-working space co-founded by Henry Faber, Jennie Faber, and Dann Toliver. Former designers and developers, the trio sought to create a productive and collaborative environment for individuals, freelancers, and small businesses that share common ideals. Since 2012, Bento Miso has hosted just under 800 open to the public events and has grown to over 138 members.

The Cool

Bento Miso is collaborative workspace in the heart of Queen West, Toronto. The space is home to Canadian indie game devs Asteroid Base (creators of the highly anticipated Lovers In A Dangerous Spacetime) and Golden Gear Games (creators of Fate Tectonics). Bento Miso offers its members access to office spaces, kitchens, a library, and meeting rooms, as well as workshops, game jams, hackathons, tech meetups, exhibits, arcades and more. The space will also offer low or no-cost memberships to anyone who asks. Its diverse population of game makers, web and software developers, entrepreneurs, and freelance makes Bento Miso the most established exciting co-working space for emerging game makers.
