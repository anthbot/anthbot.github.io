新たな現実 ゲーム教育

A New Reality
For Games Education

At The NYU Games Center in NYC

The NYU Games Center was established in 2008 as a part of New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts. The faculty at the Games Center includes industry veterans like Frank Lantz, Eric Zimmerman, and Ken Perlin as well as many other key players in the emerging NYC indie gaming scene. Today, the NYU Games Center continues to innovate games education by approaching the study of games based on a simple idea: games matter.

The Cool

The Game Center approaches games education as an exploration of games as a cultural form and game design as a creative practice. Students design, develop, critique, and study games on both the graduate and undergraduate level. Students are immersed in lectures, workshops, exhibitions, tournaments, and playtests featuring industry professionals and community members. Classes take place at MAGNET which is located at the NYU Brooklyn Campus. The facility boasts an open library of the world’s largest collection of digital games, a collaborative co-working space, and access to the next generation of virtual reality equipment and studio space. Whether you want to create, study, or critique games the NYU Games Center offers a unique opportunity to study games in a broader cultural context.
